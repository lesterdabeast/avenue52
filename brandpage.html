Campania International

In an era increasingly defined by lightweight materials and fast production, Campania International stands apart. Its cast stone planters and fountains carry a deliberate sense of weight—both physical and visual—drawing from classical European forms that resist trend cycles. These are pieces designed not for seasonal turnover, but for permanence.

Sunset West Provence

Sunset West’s Provence collection reflects a broader shift in outdoor design: away from overt luxury and toward something quieter, more tactile. With softened lines and materials built to weather both climate and time, the collection suggests that true comfort outdoors is less about display and more about endurance.

Safavieh Couture

Safavieh Couture leans into contrast—bold forms paired with controlled palettes, statement pieces that stop just short of excess. The collection captures a tension central to contemporary interiors: the desire to stand out, while still belonging within a cohesive space.

Pamono by Chairish

More archive than storefront, Pamono offers a curated view into the evolution of European design. Now under the Chairish umbrella, the platform brings together dealers and independent sellers, creating a marketplace where each piece carries its own history—an increasingly rare proposition in a landscape dominated by mass production.

Classic Home

Classic Home resists the urge to overdefine a space. Its collections—rooted in natural materials and global references—favor subtlety over statement, reflecting a growing preference for interiors that feel accumulated rather than assembled. The emphasis is less on impact, and more on atmosphere.

Bernhardt

Few brands illustrate the balancing act of legacy and relevance as clearly as Bernhardt. With decades of history behind it, the company continues to adapt—introducing modern forms without fully abandoning its traditional foundation. The result is a portfolio that speaks to an industry in transition.
